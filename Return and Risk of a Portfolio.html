4. Calculate Portfolio Risk¶

Portfolio Risk: $$\sigma_p^2 = w_1^2 \sigma_1^2 + w_2^2 \sigma_2^2 + w_3^2 \sigma_3^2 + 2 w_1 w_2 \sigma_{12} + 2 w_2 w_3 \sigma_{23} + 2 w_1 w_3 \sigma_{13}$$

Since, $$ \sigma_p^2 = \begin{bmatrix} w_1 & w_2 & w_3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{11} & \sigma_{12} & \sigma_{13} \\ \sigma_{21} & \sigma_{22} & \sigma_{23} \\ \sigma_{31} & \sigma_{32} & \sigma_{33} \\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} w_1 \\ w_2 \\ w_3 \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} w_1 & w_2 & w_3 \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} \sigma_{11} w_1 + \sigma_{12} w_2 + \sigma_{13} w_3 \\ \sigma_{21} w_1 + \sigma_{22} w_2 + \sigma_{23} w_3 \\ \sigma_{31} w_1 + \sigma_{32} w_2 + \sigma_{33} w_3 \\ \end{bmatrix} $$ $$ =\sigma_{11} w_1^2 + \sigma_{12} w_2 w_1 + \sigma_{13} w_3 w_1 + \sigma_{21} w_1 w_2 + \sigma_{22} w_2^2 + \sigma_{23} w_3 w_2 + \sigma_{31} w_1 w_3 + \sigma_{32} w_2 w_3 + \sigma_{33} w_3^2 $$

# Way 2:

def drawdown(returns):
    wealth_index = 1000*(1 + returns).cumprod()
    previous_peaks = wealth_index.cummax()
    drawdowns = (wealth_index - previous_peaks) / previous_peaks
    return pd.DataFrame({'Wealth': wealth_index, 'Previous peaks': previous_peaks, 'Drawdowns': drawdowns})
